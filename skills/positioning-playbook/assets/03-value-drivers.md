## Section 3 — Value Drivers

### Step A — Identify the 3 Value Drivers

First, analyze the research and identify the **three (3) most critical Value Drivers** — not features, but high-level business outcomes that resonate with the ICP.

For each candidate driver, perform a "Resonance Check":
1. Does it solve a top-tier pain point for the ICP?
2. Is there a significant "Cost of Inaction" if they don't solve it?
3. Is it something they are willing to shift budget for?

Replace any driver that fails the resonance check with a more urgent one. State the final 3 Value Drivers and a one-sentence justification for each.

### Step B — Expand Each Value Driver

For **each** of the 3 Value Drivers, produce a full narrative using this exact structure:

---

#### Value Driver: [Driver Name]

1. **Customer Challenge:** What is the high-stakes, specific business problem the ICP faces that this driver addresses?
2. **Status Quo:** How do they currently deal with this? Describe the manual, outdated, or inefficient "Old Way."
3. **Cost of Inaction:** Quantify the pain. What happens to their revenue, time, or competitive edge if they don't change?
4. **Our Distinct Advantage:** What specific capability makes this the only solution to solve this?
5. **Future State:** Describe the "New Way." What does success look like for the customer after implementing this?
6. **Proof Points:** Logical evidence, data points, or "reason to believe" that validates this claim.
7. **Discovery Prompts:** 2-3 strategic "trap questions" for sales reps — questions that help the customer discover this pain point themselves.

---

> **Constraint:** Outcome-oriented language. Trap questions must be genuinely strategic. No jargon.
