## Section 6 — Competitive Intelligence

Research and build a Comprehensive Competitive Intelligence Analysis. Identify 3-5 key competitors (direct rivals and indirect/incumbent threats).

### 1. Competitive Landscape Overview
- **Market Mapping:** Categorize competitors (e.g., "The Legacy Giants," "The Modern Disrupters," "The Niche Specialists")
- **Market Share & Velocity:** Which competitors are gaining traction? Which are losing relevance?

### 2. Detailed Competitor Profiles
For each competitor:
- **Firmographics:** Company size, HQ, funding/ownership, primary target industry
- **Core Value Proposition:** Their primary marketing hook and the "problem" they claim to solve best
- **Product & Pricing:** Core offering, key features, known pricing models (seat-based, usage-based, enterprise)
- **GTM Strategy:** Where do they win? (e.g., "High-touch enterprise sales" vs. "Product-led growth")

### 3. Comparative SWOT Analysis
For each competitor, framed **relative to our product**:

| Category | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Strengths** | What do they do exceptionally well? Where is their "home field advantage"? |
| **Weaknesses** | Where do they struggle? Common customer complaints or technical gaps? |
| **Opportunities (For Us)** | Where is their "blind spot"? How can we exploit their weaknesses? |
| **Threats (To Us)** | What moves could they make to block our growth (acquisitions, price wars)? |

### 4. "How to Win" (Battlecard Summary)
For each competitor, a 2-3 sentence Win Story:
- **When we win:** The specific scenario where we are the obvious choice
- **When they win:** The scenario where we should be cautious or expect heavy resistance

> **Constraint:** Focus on moves and perception, not feature lists. Prioritize recent data.
