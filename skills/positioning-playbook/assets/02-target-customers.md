## Section 2 — Target Customers (ICP)

Define who the product is *really* for and what they care about.

**ICP Formula:** *"Our ideal customers are **[Roles]** at **[Company Types]** who struggle with **[Primary Pain]**, and are trying to **[Primary JTBD]**."*

### Ideal Customer Profile

| Attribute | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Company Size** | *e.g., Mid-sized SaaS (50–500 employees)* |
| **Industry/Vertical** | *e.g., Fintech, Healthcare, Infrastructure* |
| **Geography** | *e.g., North America, EMEA* |
| **Key Roles** | *e.g., VP of Product, Head of Growth, Lead Architect* |

### Customer Jobs to be Done (JTBD)
- [Job 1: e.g., "Accurately forecast quarterly revenue without manual spreadsheets."]
- [Job 2: e.g., "Onboard new engineers in under 5 days."]
- [Job 3: e.g., "Identify security vulnerabilities before they reach production."]

### Pain Points & Desired Gains

| The Pain (Status Quo) | The Gain (Future State) |
| :--- | :--- |
| *What is breaking or costing them money today?* | *What is the specific relief they get?* |

### Alternate Solutions

| Alternatives | Differentiation Positioning Angle |
| :--- | :--- |
| **The Status Quo** | *"Your team is still using 3 tools and 10 hours a week to do what we do in one click."* |
| **Traditional Competitors** | *"They give you a static report; we give you a live dashboard you can use on Monday."* |
| **Manual Workarounds** | *"You're not hiring a person; you're hiring a repeatable process that doesn't quit."* |

### Buying Triggers
- **Trigger 1:** (e.g., A new executive hire who wants to "fix the process.")
- **Trigger 2:** (e.g., A failed product launch or a public data breach.)
- **Trigger 3:** (e.g., Reaching a specific headcount threshold where manual work breaks.)
