# Call Structures by Stage

Load the relevant section based on the call stage the rep specified.

---

## DISCOVERY CALL (45 min)

**Goal:** Qualify pain, confirm willingness to move, earn the right to a demo.

> "You want the buyer to hang up and think: they really understand my business and I want to keep talking."

### Pre-Call Hypothesis
Before the call, write one sentence:
> *"Based on my research, I believe their biggest pain is __________ and the outcome they care most about is __________."*

This gets tested in the first 5 minutes.

---

### PART 1 — SET THE STAGE (5 min)
**Purpose:** Establish credibility, set the agenda, give them control.

**Opening script template:**
> *"Thanks for making time — I know you came in through [how they came in] so I want to make sure this is actually useful for you, not just a product walkthrough. What I'd like to do is spend the first 15 minutes understanding what's going on in your world, share a quick point of view on what we're seeing across teams like yours, and then show you the parts of [product] that are most relevant to your situation. Does that work?"*

**Why this works:** Asking permission and framing around their situation — not your product — immediately separates you from every other vendor.

---

### PART 2 — DIAGNOSE CURRENT SITUATION (15 min)
**Purpose:** Confirm or correct your hypothesis. Surface pain in their words.

**Lead with your hypothesis:**
> *"Before I ask you a bunch of questions — I did some research ahead of this call and my guess is that you're dealing with [specific pain from research]. Am I close, or is there something more pressing?"*

**Discovery question categories to cover:**

*Current situation:*
- How are they managing [key workflow] today?
- Where does [key data/signal/asset] live and who's actually using it?
- How are [key teams] currently coordinating?

*Negative consequences (roll them around in the glass):*
- What does it mean for [role] when [pain occurs]?
- How is the current process affecting [relationship / metric / outcome]?
- What happens if [failure scenario] — how often does that come up?

*Role-specific probes:*
- Generate 2–3 questions specific to the prospect's persona and likely priorities based on their role and company research.

**Listen for:** These are your anchors for the rest of the call. Note the exact words they use — mirror them back later.

---

### PART 3 — SHARE YOUR POINT OF VIEW (5 min)
**Purpose:** Educate them on what good looks like. Plant required capabilities without pitching yet.

**Transition:**
> *"Can I share something we're seeing across a lot of [persona/team type] right now? I think it'll resonate."*

**Deliver the market insight (from positioning playbook):**
Use the product's market observations and value framework. Structure as:
- "The teams that are winning at [outcome] right now are doing a few things differently..."
- Present 3–4 pillars that describe the ideal state — without naming your product yet

**Then ask:**
> *"Does that match what you're experiencing, or is your situation different?"*

**Why this works:** You've described their world back to them using market language, not product language.

---

### PART 4 — CONNECT TO OUTCOMES (5 min)
**Purpose:** Get them to say out loud what success looks like.

Pick 2 after-scenario questions tied to their specific pain:
- *"If you could [eliminate the pain they described] — what would that mean for your [team / function / role]?"*
- *"What would it mean if you could show [their leadership] [the outcome they care about] — without [the painful manual process]?"*
- *"If [key metric] improved significantly, what would that change about how [leadership] views your function?"*

**Let them answer. Don't fill the silence.**

Reflect it back:
> *"So it sounds like the real win for you is __________ — is that right?"*

---

### PART 5 — FOCUSED DEMO / PROOF (10 min)
**Purpose:** Show only what's relevant to the pain they described. Do NOT run a full product tour.

**Frame it:**
> *"Based on what you've shared, I want to show you three specific things. I'm going to skip the parts that aren't relevant to your situation."*

Map their stated pain → relevant product capability → proof point.
Use the pain-to-demo mapping table from the positioning playbook.

Close the demo section with a proof point:
> *"A team similar to yours at [comparable company] was dealing with [same pain]. Within [timeframe] they saw [specific outcome]."*

---

### PART 6 — CLOSE FOR NEXT STEP (5 min)
**Purpose:** Leave with a committed next step, not "I'll think about it."

**Summarise in their language:**
> *"Based on what you shared today — [mirror their exact words about pain and desired outcome] — it sounds like there's a real fit here. What I'd recommend is [specific next step]."*

**Trap-setting close:**
> *"One thing I'd want you to think about before our next conversation — you mentioned [specific pain they shared]. In your current setup, what's the plan to solve that if you don't move forward with something like [product]? I ask because the teams we see struggle most are the ones that know they have the problem but wait another quarter to act on it."*

**Confirm the next step — always ask:**
- Who else needs to be in the room for the next conversation?
- What's the timeline for making a decision?
- Is there a budget cycle or business event creating urgency?

---

### SUCCESS CRITERIA — DISCOVERY
You've had a great call if:
- The prospect described their pain in their own words — and you reflected it back accurately
- They articulated what success looks like in a way that matters to them personally
- You showed only the product relevant to their situation
- You delivered at least one credible proof point
- You left with a specific, committed next step with the right people in the room
- They said some version of *"this is exactly what we're dealing with"*

## DEMO CALL (45–60 min)

**Goal:** Show current-state pain made real, build the case for change, earn executive sponsorship.

> Primary objective: Get them to visualise the after state so vividly they feel the cost of not acting.

### Pre-Call Setup
- Review discovery notes — what pain did they describe in their exact words?
- Identify which use cases to show — only the ones relevant to their situation
- Confirm who is in the room — tailor the demo to the most senior stakeholder present

---

### PART 1 — RECAP & RECONFIRM (5 min)
**Purpose:** Show you listened. Give them a chance to update context.

> *"Last time we spoke, you mentioned [key pain in their words] and [second pain]. Before I jump in — has anything changed, or is that still the core of what you're trying to solve?"*

---

### PART 2 — CURRENT STATE PAIN (10 min)
**Purpose:** Make the problem visceral before showing the solution.

Walk through the current state using their words from discovery:
- Describe the broken workflow as they live it today
- Quantify the cost if possible (time lost, pipeline missed, reps ignoring scoring, etc.)
- Reference their specific tools, team structure, or situation from research

> *"So right now, what you're dealing with is [current state pain]. And the impact of that is [negative consequence they described]. Does that capture it?"*

---

### PART 3 — USE-CASE DEMO (20 min)
**Purpose:** Show the after state for their specific situation — not a feature tour.

For each use case:
1. Set up the scenario: *"Here's the situation your [persona] faces today..."*
2. Show the product solving it: *"Here's what that looks like with [product]..."*
3. Connect to outcome: *"Which means [rep / team / leader] can now [outcome they said they wanted]..."*

Maximum 3 use cases. Quality over quantity.

---

### PART 4 — AFTER SCENARIO (10 min)
**Purpose:** Get them to describe the ideal future state in their own words.

> *"If you had a system that did [what you just showed them] — describe what your world looks like. What changes?"*

> *"Where would you be in [6 months / next quarter / next fiscal year] if this was working the way you described?"*

Positive business outcomes to anchor:
- What does this mean for their team's performance?
- What does this mean for their relationship with leadership?
- What does this mean for their own role and influence?

---

### PART 5 — PROOF & NEXT STEP (10 min)
**Purpose:** Reduce risk, confirm fit, advance the deal.

Deliver a proof point matched to their situation.
Confirm stakeholders and advance:
> *"Based on what you've seen today — who else needs to be part of this conversation before you could move forward?"*

Surface the VP Sales / Marketing / Finance stakeholder if not already in the room.

---

### SUCCESS CRITERIA — DEMO
- They described the after state in their own words
- You showed fewer than 4 features — all relevant to their stated pain
- You delivered a proof point that landed
- You identified all key stakeholders for the next conversation
- You left with a specific next step

## FOLLOW-UP / MULTI-STAKEHOLDER CALL (45 min)

**Goal:** Expand deal to all economic buyers, build consensus, de-risk the decision.

### Pre-Call Setup
- Who is new in the room? Research each person before the call.
- What objections are likely from each stakeholder?
- What did the champion say they would tell their stakeholders?

### Structure
1. **Re-establish context (5 min)** — let the champion recap for their colleagues, with your support
2. **Stakeholder-specific discovery (15 min)** — each stakeholder has different pain; find theirs
3. **Tailored value delivery (15 min)** — connect the product to each stakeholder's outcome
4. **Objection handling (5 min)** — address the most likely objection in the room proactively
5. **Commercial next step (10 min)** — timeline, budget, decision process, success criteria

---

## CLOSING CALL (30–45 min)

**Goal:** Resolve final objections, align on commercial terms, get a decision.

### Pre-Call Setup
- What is the last known objection or concern?
- What is their stated timeline and decision criteria?
- What is your walk-away position?

### Structure
1. **Confirm decision criteria (5 min)** — *"Last time we spoke, you said you'd be ready to move forward if [criteria]. Are we there?"*
2. **Address final objections (15 min)** — use the objection bank from the positioning playbook
3. **Present commercial terms (10 min)** — clear, simple, anchored to their stated ROI
4. **Confirm next step or close (10 min)** — if not closing today, get a specific date and decision owner

## OBJECTION HANDLING (All Stages)

Pull objection responses from the positioning playbook. Standard structure:

**Acknowledge:** *"I hear that — a lot of teams we talk to start there."*
**Reframe:** *"What they've found is..."*
**Redirect:** *"Can I ask what's driving that concern specifically?"*

Always end an objection response with a question — never let it be a dead end.
