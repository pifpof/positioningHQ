Pre-Call Plan

**Hypothesis going in:**

*"Based on my research, I believe their biggest pain is \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ and the outcome they care most about is \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_."*

Write this down. You'll test it in the first 5 minutes.

## **CALL STRUCTURE (45 minutes)**


### **PART 1 — SET THE STAGE (5 min)**

**Purpose:** Establish credibility, set the agenda, give them control.

**Opening:**

*"Thanks for making time — I know you came in through a demo request so I want to make sure this is actually useful for you, not just a product walkthrough. What I'd like to do is spend the first 15 minutes understanding what's going on in your world, share a quick **point of view on what we're seeing across teams like yours,** and then show you the parts of UserGems that are most relevant to your situation. Does that work?"*

**Why this works for RevOps:** RevOps hates being sold to. They've seen every demo. Asking permission and framing around their situation — not your product — immediately separates you.

--

### **PART 2 — DIAGNOSE CURRENT SITUATION (15 min)**

**Purpose:** Confirm or correct your hypothesis. Surface pain in their words.

**Start with a hypothesis, not a blank slate:**

*"Before I ask you a bunch of questions — I did some research ahead of this call and my guess is that you're dealing with \[signals scattered across tools / reps not trusting prioritization / outreach that isn't converting\]. Am I close, or is there something more pressing?"*

**Then go deeper with discovery questions:**

*Current situation:*

* "How is your team managing outbound prioritization today — is there a defined process or is it largely rep-driven?"  
* "Tell me about the signal sources you're working with right now — where does that data live and who's actually using it?"  
* "How are sales and marketing currently coordinating on target accounts — are they running off the same list?"

*Uncover the negative consequences:*

* "What does it mean for your reps when they're spending time on accounts that weren't really in-market?"  
* "Can you explain how the current process is impacting the relationship between your SDR team and the AEs they support?"  
* "What happens if a champion changes jobs and no one catches it — how often does that come up?"

*Listen for:* siloed tools, rep distrust of scoring, manual research, spray-and-pray outreach, misaligned sales and marketing, missed signals. **These are your anchors for the rest of the call.**

**RevOps-specific probes:**

* "How much of your time right now is spent maintaining integrations and data pipelines vs. actually improving the GTM motion?"  
* "When leadership asks for pipeline attribution, how confident are you in the data you're pulling?"

---

### **PART 3 — SHARE YOUR POINT OF VIEW (5 min)**

**Purpose:** Educate them on what good looks like. Plant the required capabilities without pitching yet.

**Transition:**

*"Can I share something we're seeing across a lot of RevOps teams right now? I think it'll resonate."*

**Deliver the market insight:**

*"The teams that are winning at outbound and ABM right now are doing a few things differently. **First — signal-based outreach should be easy**. Buying signals should surface automatically and be ranked, not require your reps to pull from five tools every morning. **Second — prioritization should be transparent**. If reps don't understand why an account is ranked highly, they ignore it and go back to gut feel. **Third — sales and marketing should be executing off the same signal at the same time, not running parallel plays that confuse the buyer**. And **fourth — whatever system you build, it should compound over time. You shouldn't be rebuilding your targeting logic from scratch every quarter."***

**Then ask:**

*"Does that match what you're experiencing, or is your situation different?"*

**Why this works:** **You've just described their world back** to them using market language, not product language. RevOps will either confirm it (great — proceed) or correct it (even better — you now know exactly where to focus).

--

### **PART 4 — CONNECT TO OUTCOMES (5 min)**

**Purpose:** Get them to say out loud what success looks like. This is what they'll remember after the call.

**After scenario questions — pick 2:**

* *"If you could give your reps a ranked list of accounts to call every morning — already researched, already scored, already queued in Salesforce — what would that mean for your SDR team?"*  
* *"What would it mean if you could tell your CRO exactly which accounts are in-market right now and show the signal behind it — without pulling a manual report?"*  
* *"If outbound reply rates went from 1–2% to 10%+, what would that change about how leadership views the RevOps function?"*

**Let them answer. Don't fill the silence.**

The goal is to get them to articulate the value in their own words. When they do, reflect it back:

*"So it sounds like the real win for you is \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ — is that right?"*

### **PART 5 — FOCUSED DEMO / PROOF (10 min)**

**Purpose:** Show only what's relevant to the pain they just described. Do NOT run the full product tour.

**Frame it:**

*"Based on what you've shared, I want to show you three specific things. I'm going to skip the parts that aren't relevant to your situation."*

**Map their pain to the demo:**

| If they said… | Show this… |
| ----- | ----- |
| "Reps don't know who to call" | Signal library \+ unified account scoring dashboard |
| "Scoring is a black box / reps don't trust it" | Human-in-the-loop scoring with adjustable weights |
| "AI outreach isn't converting" | Gem-E email examples \+ 6–20% reply rate proof |
| "Sales and marketing aren't aligned" | Workflow orchestration — ads \+ outreach \+ CRM in sync |
| "Too many tools, too much manual work" | Native CRM/SEP integration — reps see it in Salesforce |
| "Data quality is questionable" | Proprietary data \+ free data test offer |

**Close the demo section with a proof point:**

*"A team similar to yours at \[Docebo / Mimecast / Sendoso\] was dealing with \[same pain\]. Within \[timeframe\] they saw \[specific outcome\]. And we back all of this with a money-back guarantee — if you don't see at least 1x revenue ROI, you get a refund."*

--

### **PART 6 — CLOSE FOR NEXT STEP (5 min)**

**Purpose:** Leave with a committed next step, not a "I'll think about it."

**Summarize in their language:**

*"Based on what you shared today — \[mirror their exact words about their pain and desired outcome\] — it sounds like there's a real fit here. What I'd recommend is \[next step: technical deep dive / stakeholder call with VP Sales or Marketing / proof of concept\]."*

**Trap-setting close:**

*"One thing I'd want you to think about before our next conversation — you mentioned \[specific pain they shared\]. In your current setup, what's the plan to solve that if you don't move forward with something like UserGems? I ask because the teams we see struggle most are the ones that know they have the problem but wait another quarter to act on it."*

**Confirm the next step:**

* Who else needs to be in the room for the next conversation? (Surface the VP Sales / Marketing stakeholder)  
* What's the timeline for making a decision?  
* Is there a budget cycle or business event (new fiscal year, hiring push, product launch) creating urgency?

## **OBJECTION RESPONSES**

**"We already use \[6sense / ZoomInfo / Clay\]"**

*"A lot of our best customers came from those tools. The question isn't whether those tools have value — it's whether they give your reps one place with all the signals, transparent scoring, and AI outreach that actually converts. Most teams using those tools are still stitching it together manually. Is that what you're experiencing?"*

**"We're not sure we need another tool"**

*"That's exactly the right instinct — the last thing RevOps needs is more tools. The reason teams move to UserGems is usually the opposite: they want to replace 3–4 point solutions with one platform. What does your current stack look like?"*

**"We need to involve \[VP Sales / VP Marketing\] before going further"**

*"That makes total sense — this decision absolutely needs their buy-in. Based on what you've shared today, what do you think their biggest concern would be? I'd love to help you frame this for them before that conversation."*

## **SUCCESS CRITERIA FOR THIS CALL**

You've had a great call if:

* The prospect described their pain in their own words — and you reflected it back accurately  
* They articulated what success looks like in a way that matters to them personally  
* You showed only the product relevant to their situation — not a full tour  
* You delivered at least one proof point they found credible  
* You left with a specific, committed next step with the right people in the room  
* They said some version of *"this is exactly what we're dealing with"*
